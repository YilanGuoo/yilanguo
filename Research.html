Agenda Setting in the Wizarding World: Examining Attribute Agenda and Network Agenda in the “Hogwarts Legacy” Video Game Controversy on Twitter/X

This study analyzed the second-level and third-level agenda setting for the controversy around the new Harry Potter video game across different time phases. I led the network agenda setting part, by conducting semi-supervised machine learning with manual codings using BERT in Python and performing network agenda-setting analysis with MRQAP tests using R and UCINET. Furthermore, I created visualizations of the topic networks using Gephi.

Evaluating the Potential Use of an Online Resource Hub for Afghan Refugees

This project aims to assist MAPS-AMEN, an aid organization based in Seattle, in evaluating their newly launched online resource hub for Afghan refugees. Study 1 identified the needs of Afghan refugees through a series of interviews, analyzing how online resources can facilitate and address barriers to gratify these needs. In Study 2, we adopted the Technology Acceptance Model, combining the results of Study 1 to predict the potential use of the online resource hub by Afghan refugees. The Effect of Breaking the Fourth Wall on Viewers’ Arousal and Relatedness when Watching Video Game Recordings

I led this study by employing a factorial experimental design to investigate how the presence or absence of fourth-wall breaks and game commentary in video game recordings affect people's arousal and three types of relatedness. The experiments were conducted at the Communication Research Center at Boston University, and arousal was measured using Galvanic Skin Response with Shimmer sensors.
